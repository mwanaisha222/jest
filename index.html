About Us

Our Vision

To become Uganda's leading food processing company known for pioneering affordable, safe, and nutritious condiments that drive inclusive agribusiness growth across local and export markets.

Our Mission

To locally transform surplus and underutilized agricultural produce into high quality, affordable and innovative food products that meet consumer needs while creating value for farmers and vendors across the food system.

Business Objectives

To foster innovation in the food industry by combining science, technology and community engagement to develop unique solutions and products that substitute imported food products.

Professional Profile

Esther Nakimuli is a co-founder of Jest Foods Industries Limited and a bioprocessing engineer from Makerere University. She oversees operations, quality control, and supply chain management at Jest Foods, ensuring efficient production processes and adherence to food safety standards.

Esther spearheaded the development of Jest Foods' innovative probiotic ketchup, combining her expertise in biotechnology with a commitment to creating healthier food alternatives for Ugandan consumers. Her strategic vision focuses on scaling operations while maintaining the company's core values of sustainability and community impact.

Professional Profile

Jackline Bawaye is a co-founder of Jest Foods Industries Limited and a graduate of the School of Food Technology, Nutrition and Bioengineering at Makerere University. She brings extensive expertise in food processing and product development, having led the formulation of Jest Foods' flagship tomato ketchup product.

Her commitment to innovation and community empowerment drives Jest Foods' mission to transform Uganda's food processing landscape.

Strategic Plan 2025-2027

Projected Monthly Revenue

Profit Margin

25-30%

Current average profit margins on products

Phase 1: Certification & Efficiency (2025)

Obtain UNBS certification for all product lines, acquire pulper finisher to improve efficiency and reduce labor-intensive processing.

Budget Allocation: UGX 50 million

Phase 2: Packaging & Production (2026)

Purchase sachet packaging machine to expand into low-cost consumer packaging and secure larger, semi-automated production facility.

Budget Allocation: UGX 150 million

Phase 3: Market Expansion (2026-2027)

Scale vendor integration model to five new market groups and expand into formal supermarket chains and institutional buyers.

Budget Allocation: UGX 20 million

Phase 4: Product Innovation (2027)

Launch new product lines including appetizer sauce, pizza/pasta sauce, mayonnaise, tomato paste, and jam.

R&D Investment: UGX 30 million
